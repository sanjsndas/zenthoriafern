    🌱 Essential Fern Care Checklist

    • ☐ Light assessment (indirect / dappled)
    • ☐ Humidity > 60% (misting / pebble tray)
    • ☐ Well-draining, acidic soil
    • ☐ Fertilize half-strength in growing season
    • ☐ Monitor for scale & mealybugs
    • ☐ Repot every 2-3 years
    Comparison: Nephrolepis vs. Adiantum — Nephrolepis (Boston) tolerates drier air, Adiantum (Maidenhair) demands constant moisture and high humidity.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    How often should I water my indoor fern?

    Typically 1-2 times per week, depending on humidity. Check top inch of soil — water when dry to touch.

    What is the best fern for low light?

    Japanese Holly Fern, Bird's Nest Fern, and Button Fern are excellent low-light performers.

    Can I grow ferns from spores?

    Yes, but it requires patience and sterile technique. Our spore guide covers every step.

    Why are my fern fronds turning brown?

    Usually due to low humidity, inconsistent watering, or direct sunlight. Adjust microclimate.
